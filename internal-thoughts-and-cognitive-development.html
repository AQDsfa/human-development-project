Internal Thoughts & Cognitive Development

During Infancy and Childhood

Internal thoughts during infancy and childhood are primarily shaped by sensory experiences and early interactions. Cognitive development involves the formation of basic concepts and the foundation for language acquisition.

During Adolescence

Internal thoughts become more complex as adolescents develop abstract thinking and begin to form a sense of identity. Cognitive development involves critical thinking, problem-solving, and exploring personal beliefs and values.

During Adulthood

Internal thoughts in adulthood are influenced by experiences, responsibilities, and personal growth. Cognitive development continues with lifelong learning, adaptation to challenges, and refining decision-making skills.
